Mendoza in Papua New Guinea!

The story of MENDOZAA! starts in Papua New Guinea. Mendoza did not have access to internet therefore had very little knowledge of technology. His first few experiences in computer science was from his aunt in Australia who he used to visit a lot who was a front-end web developer.

Without the proper education or experience in IT he was a bit hopeless.

Mendoza in Australia

Moving to australia in 2016 Mendoza finally got access to a computer. He was very interested by it often downloading random things off the internet.

Still never had much experience in programming.

Mendoza in High School

The story of MENDOZA! and programming in high school began when he had to pick his year 11 elective subjects. He chose software design and development on an off chance for no particular reason. He failed every other subject except this subject.

This is where MENDOZA! got his start in many things, such as first learning python, then javascript+html, moving on to C#, then eventually golang and haskell. His formitive growth was here, making a lot of friends, one who introduced him to linux as a hobby.

Now mendoza uses vim as his main text editor and doesn't have to lift his hands off his keyboard (he doesn't have to use a mouse) in order to operate his computer due to using a tiling window manager called xmonad.

MENDOZAA had many difficult assignments during highschool. He had his first experience using databases with postgres and sqlite, he had his experiences using winforms with C# as a hangman game, and doing Object Oriented Programming. Then he went on to move towards C++. His final assignment was a full-stack website using graphql, golang, typescript and react.

Mendoza at Macquarie University

When MENDOZAA graduated highschool, he didn't get a very good ATAR probably because of his lack of effort. Hence, All of his friend's from highschool moved to UTS and he was stuck alone at Macquarie University.

The teachers and student's at Macquarie Uni were very nice, it was here Mendoza first started looking at graphics programming, and where he started using rust as his primary programming language. He joined the google developer club and started exploring functional programming and system administration more. One of his lecturer's ran a weekly soccer game, and that was fun.

MENDOZAAS discrete maths lecturer used to use Haskell all the time during the lesson, and thus intrigued him. He sucked at Maths up until university but left him at a cross roads. Currently studying science, he wasn't liking his degree. He was left wondering whether to go to UTS with his friends and do computer science, or whether to change his major to maths from computing, and then do a double degree in information systems as well. He eventually chose to go to UTS.
